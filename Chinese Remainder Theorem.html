Chinese Remainder Theorem

The Chinese Remainder Theorem is a number theory result that states that if you know the remainders of the division of an integer by several pairwise coprime integers, then you can determine the remainder of the division of that integer by the product of these integers.

Example:

Suppose we have the following system of congruences:

  • x ≡ 2 (mod 3)
  • x ≡ 3 (mod 5)
  • x ≡ 2 (mod 7)

What is x?

The answer according to CRT: The solution is x = 23

Because

  • 23/3 has a remainder of 2
  • 23/5 has a remainder of 3
  • 23/7 has a remainder of 2
